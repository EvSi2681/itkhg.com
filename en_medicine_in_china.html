Modern Medicine in China: How People in the Middle Kingdom Approach Healthcare

When we think of Chinese medicine, acupuncture, herbal remedies, and qigong often come to mind. However, behind this traditional image lies a highly developed, technology-driven healthcare system grounded in evidence-based medicine.

In China, modern (Western-style) medicine forms the backbone of clinical practice. Hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies nationwide rely on registered pharmaceutical products developed according to international scientific standards. Chinese citizens trust and widely use these medications—for everything from common colds to chronic conditions.

Leading Chinese pharmaceutical companies—such as Sinopharm, Fosun Pharma, and Hengrui Medicine—produce millions of medicine packages annually. All products undergo strict registration, clinical trials, and quality control in compliance with China’s national Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) standards.

Below is an overview of medications commonly used in China’s modern healthcare system. This information is provided for educational purposes only and reflects standard medical practice in the People’s Republic of China.

Medications Commonly Used in China’s Healthcare System

速效救心丸 (Suxiao Jiuxin Wan)

Primary use: Symptomatic relief during angina attacks.

Pharmacological action: Rapid dilation of coronary arteries, improving myocardial blood flow.

Russian equivalent: Nitroglycerin.

Registered in China and widely used as an emergency remedy for chest pain. Effects begin within 2–3 minutes after sublingual administration. Manufactured according to China’s pharmaceutical standards.

苯磺酸氨氯地平片 (Amlodipine Besylate)

Primary use: Hypertension and stable angina.

Pharmacological action: Calcium channel blocker; reduces peripheral vascular resistance.

Russian equivalent: Amlodipine, Norvasc.

One of the most commonly prescribed antihypertensive drugs in China. Taken once daily for 24-hour blood pressure control. Produced by major manufacturers including CSPC and Sino-American Shanghai Squibb.

甲巯咪唑片 (Thiamazole)

Primary use: Hyperthyroidism, Graves’ disease.

Pharmacological action: Inhibits thyroid hormone synthesis.

Russian equivalent: Mercaptopurine (Mercazole).

Standard treatment for hyperthyroidism in China. Prescribed under medical supervision with regular hormone monitoring. Available by prescription in public and private clinics.

盐酸舍曲林片 (Sertraline Hydrochloride)

Primary use: Depression, anxiety disorders, OCD, panic attacks.

Pharmacological action: Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI).

Russian equivalent: Zoloft, Sertraline.

Widely used in Chinese psychiatric practice due to proven efficacy and good tolerability. Prescribed in treatment courses under specialist supervision.

蒙脱石散 (Montmorillonite Powder)

Primary use: Acute and chronic diarrhea of various causes.

Pharmacological action: Adsorbs toxins, viruses, and bacteria; protects the gastrointestinal mucosa.

Russian equivalent: Smecta.

Safe for adults and children, not absorbed into the bloodstream. Used as part of comprehensive treatment for intestinal infections. Supplied as a powder for oral suspension.

京都念慈菴蜜炼川贝枇杷膏 (Nin Jiom Pei Pa Koa)

Primary use: Symptomatic relief of cough associated with colds and bronchitis.

Pharmacological action: Expectorant, soothing, anti-inflammatory.

Russian equivalent: Herbion, ivy-based syrups.

Combines traditional Chinese herbal formulas with modern manufacturing standards. Produced by “Nin Jiom,” a company with over 300 years of heritage. Pleasant-tasting and suitable for adults and children.

Bayer Low Dose Aspirin 81 mg

Primary use: Prevention of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke).

Pharmacological action: Antiplatelet agent—inhibits platelet aggregation.

Russian equivalent: Cardiomagnil, Aspecard.

Although developed in Europe, this product is available in China through international pharmacy networks and e-commerce platforms. Local low-dose acetylsalicylic acid generics are also widely used in China.

劲通 双氯芬酸钠气雾剂 (Diclofenac Sodium Aerosol)

Primary use: Local relief of pain and inflammation from sprains, arthritis, and muscle aches.

Pharmacological action: Topical non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).

Russian equivalent: Diclofenac spray, Voltaren Emulgel.

A popular household and sports remedy in China. Provides fast pain and swelling relief without gastrointestinal exposure. Convenient for acute injuries or chronic joint pain.
