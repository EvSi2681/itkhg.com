Modern Medicine in China: How People in the Middle Kingdom Approach Healthcare

When we think of Chinese medicine, acupuncture, herbal remedies, and qigong often come to mind. However, behind this traditional image lies a highly developed, technology-driven healthcare system grounded in evidence-based medicine.

In China, modern (Western-style) medicine forms the backbone of clinical practice. Hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies nationwide rely on registered pharmaceutical products developed according to international scientific standards. Chinese citizens trust and widely use these medications—for everything from common colds to chronic conditions.

Leading Chinese pharmaceutical companies—such as Sinopharm, Fosun Pharma, and Hengrui Medicine—produce millions of medicine packages annually. All products undergo strict registration, clinical trials, and quality control in compliance with China’s national Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) standards.

Below is an overview of medications commonly used in China’s modern healthcare system. This information is provided for educational purposes only and reflects standard medical practice in the People’s Republic of China.

速效救心丸 — Su Xiao Jiu Xin Wan

Transliteration: Sù Xiào Jiù Xīn Wán

Name meaning: "Rapid-Effect Heart-Saving Pills" — from "Su Xiao" (rapid effect), "Jiu" (save), and "Xin" (heart).

Manufacturer: Tianjin Daren Tang Pharmaceutical Group Co., Ltd. (No. 6 Traditional Chinese Medicine Factory).

Registration Number: 国药准字Z12020025 — officially registered in China.

Form: Pills, 60 pills per blister (2 blisters per box). OTC product.

Description: A TCM formula designed for emergency relief of angina and heart attack symptoms. Traditionally used for "Blood stasis" in the heart, causing chest pain and discomfort.

Use for: Commonly used for:

  • Chest pain, especially during physical exertion
  • Feeling of pressure or squeezing in the chest
  • Panic, fear of death during an attack
  • Shortness of breath, sweating, pallor
  • Prevention before physical activity
Improves blood flow in coronary arteries, dissolves clots, relieves pain.

Contraindications: Do not use during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Avoid during acute inflammation. Not a substitute for emergency medical care. In case of heart attack symptoms — call emergency services immediately!

Dietary Supplement. Not a medicine. For emergency relief of angina symptoms. In case of heart attack — call emergency services!

苯磺酸氨氯地平片 — Amlodipine Besilate Tablets

Transliteration: Běn Huáng Suān Ān Lǜ Dì Píng Piàn

Name meaning: "Amlodipine Besilate Tablets" — the chemical name of the active ingredient.

Manufacturer: Jingxin Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd.

Registration Number: 国药准字H20103356 — officially registered medicine in China (H indicates a chemical drug).

Form: Tablets, 5mg × 14 tablets × 2 blisters. Prescription-only medication (not OTC).

Description: A modern pharmaceutical belonging to the calcium channel blocker group. Used for treating hypertension and stable angina pectoris. Works by relaxing smooth muscle in blood vessels, which lowers blood pressure and improves blood flow to the heart.

Use for: Commonly used for:

  • Hypertension (high blood pressure)
  • Stable angina pectoris
  • Reducing cardiac workload in ischemic heart disease
Dosage is individually prescribed by a doctor and adjusted gradually.

Contraindications: Do not use if allergic to amlodipine or any ingredient. Contraindicated in severe heart failure, aortic stenosis, or hypotension. Do not combine with other antihypertensive drugs without medical advice. Always consult a cardiologist before use.

Prescription Medicine. Requires a prescription. For treating hypertension and ischemic heart disease.

赛治® Thyrozol® 10 mg — Jia Xiu Mi Zuo Pian

Transliteration: Jiǎ Xiū Mí Zuò Piàn

Name meaning: "Methimazole Tablets" — from the chemical name of the active ingredient: Methimazole.

Manufacturer: Merck — Международная фармацевтическая компания.

Registration Number: 国药准字H20205041 — officially registered as a medicine in China.

Form: Tablets, 50 tablets per blister. Prescription-only (Rx).

Description: A pharmaceutical drug used to treat hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid gland). The active ingredient, methimazole, blocks the synthesis of thyroid hormones (T3 and T4).

Use for: Commonly used for:

  • Hyperthyroidism (thyrotoxicosis)
  • Graves' disease (diffuse toxic goiter)
  • Toxic nodular goiter
  • Preparation for thyroid surgery
  • Preparation for radioactive iodine treatment
Normalizes thyroid hormone levels, reduces symptoms of thyrotoxicosis.

Contraindications: Do not use during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Avoid during acute infections. Do not combine with other thyroid treatments without medical advice. Always consult an endocrinologist before use.

Medicine. Requires prescription. For treating hyperthyroidism and Graves' disease.

Transliteration: Yán Suān Shè Qū Lín Piàn

Name meaning: "Sertraline Hydrochloride Tablets" — from "Yan Suan" (hydrochloride) and "She Qu Lin" (sertraline).

Manufacturer: VIATRIS — Viatris Pharmaceutical Company.

Registration Number: Not shown on packaging (this is a Western drug, registration is country-specific).

Form: Tablets, 14 tablets per blister. Prescription only.

Description: A synthetic pharmaceutical belonging to the class of selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs). Used for treating depression, panic attacks, obsessive-compulsive disorder, and other mental health conditions.

Use for: Prescribed by a doctor for:

  • Depression (moderate to severe)
  • Panic attacks, anxiety disorders
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Social phobia, PTSD
  • PMDD (Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder)
Improves mood, reduces anxiety, normalizes sleep.

Contraindications: Do not take without a doctor's prescription. Do not combine with other antidepressants without professional advice. Avoid if allergic to any ingredient. Always consult a psychiatrist before use.

蒙脱石散 (Montmorillonite Powder)

Primary use: Acute and chronic diarrhea of various causes.

Pharmacological action: Adsorbs toxins, viruses, and bacteria; protects the gastrointestinal mucosa.

Russian equivalent: Smecta.

Safe for adults and children, not absorbed into the bloodstream. Used as part of comprehensive treatment for intestinal infections. Supplied as a powder for oral suspension.

京都念慈菴蜜炼川贝枇杷膏 (Nin Jiom Pei Pa Koa)

Primary use: Symptomatic relief of cough associated with colds and bronchitis.

Pharmacological action: Expectorant, soothing, anti-inflammatory.

Russian equivalent: Herbion, ivy-based syrups.

Combines traditional Chinese herbal formulas with modern manufacturing standards. Produced by “Nin Jiom,” a company with over 300 years of heritage. Pleasant-tasting and suitable for adults and children.

Bayer Low Dose Aspirin 81 mg

Primary use: Prevention of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke).

Pharmacological action: Antiplatelet agent—inhibits platelet aggregation.

Russian equivalent: Cardiomagnil, Aspecard.

Although developed in Europe, this product is available in China through international pharmacy networks and e-commerce platforms. Local low-dose acetylsalicylic acid generics are also widely used in China.

劲通 双氯芬酸钠气雾剂 (Diclofenac Sodium Aerosol)

Primary use: Local relief of pain and inflammation from sprains, arthritis, and muscle aches.

Pharmacological action: Topical non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).

Russian equivalent: Diclofenac spray, Voltaren Emulgel.

A popular household and sports remedy in China. Provides fast pain and swelling relief without gastrointestinal exposure. Convenient for acute injuries or chronic joint pain.
